Undergrad Projects & Publications

Simulation of Objects in Unity for Learning

SOUL, a web application developed as part of our final year evaluation for the Bachelor of Technology degree, stands out as a prime example of using interactive simulation for educational purposes. Designed to simulate rigid bodies, the application allows users to control and manipulate properties such as weight, volume, and mass, observing the corresponding changes in real-time. This project was a collaborative effort, undertaken with my peers Aakil Mathur, Anuraj Kathait, and Lavina Nagpal.

Our primary aim was to transform traditional learning methodologies, introducing a more engaging and interactive approach. By enabling users to interact directly with simulated objects, we fostered a learning environment where theoretical concepts became tangible and easier to grasp.

Our journey began with a comprehensive review of existing educational technologies, culminating in the publication ofAn Overview: Paradigm Shift of Techniques used for Educational Purposes. This paper delved into the shift towards visual and interactive learning tools, highlighting the advantages of simulation using a physics engine. Building on this foundation, we developed SOUL, detailing our process and findings in the paper SOUL: Simulation of Objects in Unity for Learning. This publication provided an in-depth exploration of our system design, prototype, and the integration of Unity for an enriched e-learning experience.

Leveraging Unity and C# for script writing, we embedded the simulation into a web application, ensuring accessibility and ease of use. Our collaborative spirit was evident throughout the project, with each member bringing unique strengths to the table, resulting in a successful and innovative educational tool.
