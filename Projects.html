Geolocation in Banking (Hackathon Project)
As part of a dynamic team of four during an IBM Client Hackathon, I contributed to the development of a Proof of Concept (POC) leveraging geolocation technology to enhance user authentication in banking scenarios. Our solution utilized OpenStreetMap, Node.js, and IBM Cloud to create a system that not only provided an additional layer of security at banks and ATMs but also dispatched relevant SMS alerts to users. This project demonstrated our team's ability to innovate within the financial technology space, showcasing our combined skills in software development and our commitment to enhancing customer security and experience.

Technologies Used: OpenStreetMap, Node.js, IBM Cloud.

Undergrad Projects & Publications

Simulation of Objects in Unity for Learning

SOUL, a web application developed as part of our final year evaluation for the Bachelor of Technology degree. This application was designed to simulates rigid body physics, allowing users to adjust properties like weight, volume, and mass in real-time. It's a collaborative creation by myself, Aakil Mathur, Anuraj Kathait, and Lavina Nagpal, aimed at transforming traditional learning by making theoretical concepts tangible through interactive simulation.

The project was inspired by our research on modern educational technologies, particularly the shift towards visual and interactive methods. Our findings were published in "An Overview: Paradigm Shift of Techniques used for Educational Purposes" which led to the development of SOUL and a subsequent paper "SOUL: Simulation of Objects in Unity for Learning" detailing our approach and findings. 

Using Unity3D, C#, JavaScript for scripting, we integrated the simulation into a web application, focusing on accessibility and user experience. Our team's diverse skills contributed to creating this educational platform.

Technologies Used: Unity3D, JavaScript, HTML, CSS.

Check out the project on GitHub!
